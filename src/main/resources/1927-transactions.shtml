All Transactions

Transactions listed are from November 1 of previous year to April 1 of the following year.

  • November 3, 1926

    The Boston Braves selected Jack Knight off waivers from the Philadelphia Phillies.

  • November 4, 1926

    The Brooklyn Robins released Jack Fournier.

  • November 11, 1926

    Shreveport (Texas) purchased Spence Harris from the Chicago White Sox.

    Wichita Falls (Texas) purchased Milt Steengrafe from the Detroit Tigers.

  • November 23, 1926

    The St. Louis Cardinals signed Rabbit Maranville as a free agent.

  • November 27, 1926

    Kansas City (American Association) traded Dud Branom to the Philadelphia Athletics for Bill Wambsganss and $50,000.

  • December, 1926

    Dallas (Texas) purchased Charlie Berry from the Philadelphia Athletics.

    Memphis (Southern Association) purchased Charlie Engle from the Philadelphia Athletics.

    Waterbury (Eastern) purchased Jim Keesey from the Philadelphia Athletics.

    Atlanta (Southern Association) purchased Frank Welch from the Philadelphia Athletics.

    The Boston Red Sox traded players to be named later to St Paul (American Association) for Fred Hofmann and Pee-Wee Wanninger. The Boston Red Sox sent Al Stokes (November, 1926), Fred Heimach (December, 1926), Paul Zahniser (December, 1926) and Alex Gaston (December, 1926) to St Paul (American Association) to complete the trade.

  • December 20, 1926

    The New York Giants traded Frankie Frisch and Jimmy Ring to the St. Louis Cardinals for Rogers Hornsby.

  • December 21, 1926

    The New York Yankees traded $40000 and 2 players to be named to St Paul (American Association) for Julie Wera.

  • December 23, 1926

    The Philadelphia Athletics signed Eddie Collins as a free agent.

  • December 27, 1926

    The Boston Braves signed Jack Fournier as a free agent.

  • Before 1927 Season

    The Philadelphia Athletics returned Tom Jenkins to Wichita Falls (Texas) as part of a conditional deal.

    The Philadelphia Athletics purchased Joe Hassler from Wheeling (Mid-Atlantic).

    St Paul (American Association) purchased Herb McQuaid from the New York Yankees.

    The Philadelphia Athletics purchased Bill Shores from Wichita Falls (Texas).

  • 10, 1927

    Leo Mangum sent from Portland (PCL) to the Chicago White Sox in an unknown transaction.

  • January 1, 1927

    The Brooklyn Robins released Zack Wheat.

  • January 9, 1927

    As part of a 3-team trade: The New York Giants sent Jack Scott and Fresco Thompson to the Philadelphia Phillies. The Philadelphia Phillies sent George Harper to the New York Giants. The Philadelphia Phillies sent Butch Henline to the Brooklyn Robins. The Brooklyn Robins sent Burleigh Grimes to the New York Giants.

  • January 12, 1927

    The Philadelphia Athletics signed Zack Wheat as a free agent.

  • January 13, 1927

    The Chicago White Sox traded Johnny Grabowski and Ray Morehart to the New York Yankees for Aaron Ward.

  • January 15, 1927

    The Detroit Tigers traded Otis Miller, Billy Mullen, Frank O'Rourke and Lefty Stewart to the St. Louis Browns for Pinky Hargrave, Bobby LaMotte and Marty McManus.

    The Chicago White Sox traded Leo Mangum and Sloppy Thurston to the Washington Senators for Roger Peckinpaugh.

  • January 31, 1927

    The Cleveland Indians released Tris Speaker.

    The Washington Senators signed Tris Speaker as a free agent.

  • February 4, 1927

    The Pittsburgh Pirates selected Joe Harris off waivers from the Washington Senators.

  • February 8, 1927

    Ty Cobb assigned by the Detroit Tigers to the Philadelphia Athletics.

    The New York Yankees traded Sad Sam Jones to the St. Louis Browns for Cedric Durst and Joe Giard.

  • February 9, 1927

    The Cincinnati Reds traded Edd Roush to the New York Giants for High Pockets Kelly and cash.

  • February 15, 1927

    Pittsfield (Eastern) purchased Sid Womack from the Boston Braves.

  • March 29, 1927

    The Washington Senators purchased Mickey O'Neil from the Brooklyn Robins.

    Baltimore (International) purchased Leo Mangum and Curly Ogden from the Washington Senators.

  • April 9, 1927

    The Philadelphia Athletics traded to Wichita Falls (Texas) for unknown compensation. Alex Metzler returned to original team on April 9, 1927.

  • April 10, 1927

    Portland (PCL) purchased Frank Sigafoos from the Philadelphia Athletics.

  • April 23, 1927

    The Chicago White Sox purchased Alex Metzler from Wichita Falls (Texas).

  • May, 1927

    The Brooklyn Robins returned Oscar Roettger (earlier draft pick) to St Paul (American Association).

  • May 2, 1927

    The Boston Red Sox traded Topper Rigney to the Washington Senators for Buddy Myer.

  • May 3, 1927

    The Brooklyn Robins traded Bob McGraw to the St. Louis Cardinals for Jake Flowers.

  • May 9, 1927

    The New York Giants purchased Don Songer from the Pittsburgh Pirates.

  • May 25, 1927

    The New York Giants purchased Mickey O'Neil from the Washington Senators.

  • June, 1927

    The Philadelphia Athletics signed Neal Baker as an amateur free agent.

    Newark (International) purchased Jack Bentley from the New York Giants.

    Baltimore (International) purchased Guy Cantrell from the Brooklyn Robins.

  • June 1, 1927

    Portland (PCL) purchased Dud Branom from the Philadelphia Athletics. (Date given is approximate. Exact date is uncertain.)

  • June 7, 1927

    The Chicago Cubs traded Jimmy Cooney and Tony Kaufmann to the Philadelphia Phillies for Hal Carlson.

  • June 12, 1927

    The Cleveland Indians purchased Baby Doll Jacobson from the Boston Red Sox.

    The Boston Braves traded Larry Benton, Zack Taylor and Herb Thomas to the New York Giants for Doc Farrell, Kent Greenfield and Hugh McQuillan.

  • June 14, 1927

    The Chicago Cubs purchased Wayland Dean from the Philadelphia Phillies.

  • June 15, 1927

    The Chicago White Sox purchased Bernie Neis from the Cleveland Indians.

    The Pittsburgh Pirates released Bullet Joe Bush.

  • June 17, 1927

    The Washington Senators released Stan Coveleski.

  • June 18, 1927

    The Boston Braves signed Dinny McNamara as a free agent.

  • June 28, 1927

    The New York Giants signed Bullet Joe Bush as a free agent.

  • July 11, 1927

    The Boston Red Sox purchased Frank Welch from Atlanta (Southern Association).

  • July 12, 1927

    The Washington Senators signed Lefty Atkinson as a free agent.

    The Chicago Cubs purchased Fred Haney from the Boston Red Sox.

  • July 18, 1927

    The New York Giants selected Les Mann off waivers from the Boston Braves.

  • July 19, 1927

    The New York Giants released Bullet Joe Bush.

    The Boston Red Sox released Rudy Sommers.

    The Washington Senators selected Ed Wells off waivers from the Detroit Tigers.

    The Washington Senators selected Lefty Willis off waivers from the Philadelphia Athletics.

  • July 20, 1927

    The Washington Senators returned Lefty Willis to the Philadelphia Athletics following previous waiver selection.

  • July 23, 1927

    The Boston Braves purchased Sid Graves from Lynn (New England).

    Birmingham (Southern Association) purchased Ed Wells from the Washington Senators.

  • July 24, 1927

    The Brooklyn Robins purchased Nap Kloza from Birmingham (Southern Association).

  • July 26, 1927

    Fort Worth (Texas) purchased Joe Pate from the Philadelphia Athletics.

    The New York Giants purchased Buck Jordan from Charlotte (South Atlantic).

  • August 5, 1927

    The Philadelphia Athletics selected Baby Doll Jacobson off waivers from the Cleveland Indians.

  • August 8, 1927

    The New York Yankees traded $20000 and a player to be named to St Paul (American Association) for Gene Robertson.

    The Washington Senators selected Bill Lamar off waivers from the Philadelphia Athletics.

  • August 9, 1927

    The Chicago Cubs traded Fred Haney and cash to Indianapolis (American Association) for Elmer Yoter.

  • August 12, 1927

    The New York Yankees traded $125,000 to Oakland (PCL) for Lyn Lary and Jimmie Reese.

  • August 15, 1927

    The Boston Braves purchased Earl Clark from Richmond (Virginia).

  • August 26, 1927

    The Brooklyn Robins purchased Al Lopez from Jacksonville (Southeastern) for $10,000.

  • August 29, 1927

    The Boston Braves purchased Guy Morrison from Waterbury (Eastern).

  • August 30, 1927

    The Cincinnati Reds released Art Nehf.

  • August 31, 1927

    The Boston Braves purchased Ed Brandt from Seattle (PCL).

  • September, 1927

    Baltimore (International) sent Guy Cantrell to the Philadelphia Athletics as part of a conditional deal.

  • September 1, 1927

    The Chicago Cubs signed Art Nehf as a free agent.

  • September 2, 1927

    The Boston Braves purchased Charlie Fitzberger from Providence (Eastern).

  • September 3, 1927

    The Brooklyn Robins traded a player to be named later and cash and three players to be named to Milwaukee (American Association) for Harry Riconda. The Brooklyn Robins sent Johnny Butler (December 15, 1927) to Milwaukee (American Association) to complete the trade.

  • September 10, 1927

    The St. Louis Cardinals purchased Tony Kaufmann from the Philadelphia Phillies.

    Buffalo (International) traded Ty Tyson to the Brooklyn Robins for players to be named later. The Brooklyn Robins sent Bob Barrett (December 15, 1927) and Gus Felix (December 15, 1927) to Buffalo (International) to complete the trade.

  • September 13, 1927

    The Washington Senators purchased Babe Ganzel from Birmingham (Southern Association).

  • September 14, 1927

    The Boston Red Sox purchased Charlie Berry from Dallas (Texas).

    The Boston Red Sox purchased Paul Hinson from Joplin (Western Association).

  • September 17, 1927

    The New York Giants selected Norman Plitt off waivers from the Brooklyn Robins.

    The Chicago White Sox drafted Johnny Mann from Wichita Falls (Texas) in the 1927 rule 5 draft.

    The Chicago Cubs drafted Norm McMillan from St Paul (American Association) in the 1927 rule 5 draft.

    The Boston Braves drafted Earl Williams from Ashville (South Atlantic) in the 1927 rule 5 draft.

    The Cleveland Indians drafted Frank Wilson from Milwaukee (American Association) in the 1927 rule 5 draft.

    The Brooklyn Robins signed Dave Bancroft as a free agent.

    The Boston Braves released Dave Bancroft.

    The St. Louis Browns traded Milt Gaston and Sad Sam Jones to the Washington Senators for Dick Coffman and Earl McNeely.

  • November 5, 1927
